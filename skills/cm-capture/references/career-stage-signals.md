# Career Stage Signals

Directional signals for adapting behavior based on `career_stage` from context.md. Read this when career_stage is set and use it to adjust emphasis across capture, assess, craft, and interview interactions.

## Stage Adaptation Matrix

| Stage | Capture Emphasis | Questioning Direction | Output Weighting |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| graduate | Translate academic/internship into professional language; help identify transferable skills from coursework | "What did you learn? What was your approach?" Focus on learning velocity and potential | Growth trajectory > specific achievements; emphasize learning ability and foundational skills |
| mid-career | Depth articulation — help articulate what they've been doing well but may take for granted | "What was your specific contribution? What decisions did you drive?" Probe for ownership signals | Balance of technical depth + business impact; demonstrate consistent delivery |
| senior/lead | Scope-of-influence narrative — move beyond project-level detail to organizational impact | "Who else was involved? What was the organizational impact? How did this change how others work?" | Strategic influence > execution detail; demonstrate level-above behavior |
| career-change | Transferable skill mapping — find bridges between old and new domains; reframe non-obvious experience as assets | "What from your previous experience applies here? What perspective does your background give you?" | Adaptability + cross-domain value > domain depth; emphasize unique perspective |

For stages not listed (active-seeking, exploring, other), apply balanced weighting across all axes.

## Usage

This file is a reference, not a script. Use the signals above to adjust your natural interaction style — do not mechanically enumerate stages to the user. The career_stage value should subtly shape your questions, your assessment emphasis, and your content recommendations without being explicitly mentioned unless relevant.
